Medical Medications by Specialty

Anesthesiology

  • Propofol (sedative for anesthesia)
  • Lidocaine (local anesthetic)
  • Fentanyl (pain management)

Gastroenterology

  • Omeprazole (for acid reflux)
  • Mesalamine (for inflammatory bowel diseases)
  • Loperamide (anti-diarrheal)

Obstetrics and Gynecology

  • Oxytocin (induces labor)
  • Clomiphene (fertility treatment)
  • Mifepristone (for medical abortion)

Cardiology

  • Aspirin (antiplatelet, reduces heart attack risk)
  • Atorvastatin (cholesterol-lowering)
  • Metoprolol (beta-blocker for hypertension)

ENT (Ear, Nose, and Throat)

  • Amoxicillin (antibiotic for ear/nose/throat infections)
  • Naphazoline (nasal decongestant)
  • Ciprofloxacin (antibiotic for ear infections)

General Surgery

  • Cefazolin (prophylactic antibiotic before surgery)
  • Heparin (anticoagulant)
  • Morphine (pain relief post-surgery)

Internal Medicine

  • Metformin (for type 2 diabetes)
  • Lisinopril (for high blood pressure)
  • Prednisone (anti-inflammatory for a range of conditions)

Pediatrics

  • Amoxicillin (common pediatric antibiotic)
  • Acetaminophen (fever and pain in children)
  • Albuterol (for pediatric asthma)

Cardiothoracic Surgery

  • Clopidogrel (antiplatelet post-heart surgery)
  • Warfarin (anticoagulant)
  • Nitroglycerin (for heart disease)

Dentistry

  • Lidocaine (local anesthetic for dental procedures)
  • Amoxicillin (antibiotic for infections)
  • Ibuprofen (pain relief after dental procedures)

Dermatology

  • Hydrocortisone (for eczema and rashes)
  • Isotretinoin (for severe acne)
  • Antihistamines (for allergic skin reactions)

Emergency Medicine

  • Epinephrine (for anaphylaxis)
  • Naloxone (for opioid overdose)
  • Atropine (for bradycardia)

Neurology

  • Levodopa (for Parkinson’s disease)
  • Gabapentin (for neuropathic pain)
  • Phenytoin (for seizures)

Plastic Surgery

  • Botulinum toxin (for cosmetic procedures)
  • Silicone gel (for scar management)
  • Tranexamic acid (to reduce bleeding in surgeries)

Psychiatry

  • Sertraline (antidepressant)
  • Lithium (for bipolar disorder)
  • Risperidone (antipsychotic)

Diagnostic Radiology

  • Iodinated contrast agents (for CT scans)
  • Gadolinium (for MRI contrast)
  • Technetium-99m (for nuclear imaging)

Anatomy

Not typically associated with medications; focuses on structural studies of the human body.

Geriatrics

  • Donepezil (for Alzheimer’s disease)
  • Furosemide (for heart failure)
  • Vitamin D supplements (for osteoporosis)

Endocrinology

  • Insulin (for diabetes)
  • Levothyroxine (for hypothyroidism)
  • Hydrocortisone (for adrenal insufficiency)

Infectious Diseases

  • Amoxicillin-clavulanate (broad-spectrum antibiotic)
  • Oseltamivir (for influenza)
  • Hydroxychloroquine (for malaria)

Allergies

  • Cetirizine (antihistamine)
  • Epinephrine (for severe allergic reactions)
  • Montelukast (for allergy-induced asthma)

Blood Bank

  • Erythropoietin (stimulates red blood cell production)
  • Anticoagulants (prevent blood clotting during storage)
  • Immunoglobulin (for immune deficiencies)

Intensive Care Medicine

  • Norepinephrine (for blood pressure support)
  • Fentanyl (for pain relief in critically ill patients)
  • Propofol (sedation in ICU)

Hematology

  • Ferrous sulfate (for iron-deficiency anemia)
  • Warfarin (for clotting disorders)
  • Hydroxyurea (for sickle cell disease)
